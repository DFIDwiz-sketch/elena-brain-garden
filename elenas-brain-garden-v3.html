👨‍👩‍👧 How this app helps Elena

Built around her WISC-V profile: visual maths (quantitative reasoning), pattern puzzles (fluid reasoning), listening memory (auditory working memory), vocabulary and grammar (verbal comprehension), open conversation cards, and gentle no-"wrong" feedback everywhere (fear of mistakes).

📏 NSW Stage 3 coverage

Maths Picture Lab now works by TOPIC — tap a chip under the game (or play Mixed): multiplication, division, fractions, decimals & percentages, time, length, mass, capacity, area & perimeter, money, chance and patterns. Every problem is generated fresh with new numbers, so each topic has an endless supply. Word Wizard holds 280 words in three tiers (Tier 1 everyday, Tier 2 academic, Tier 3 subject words from science, geography, maths and civics). Grammar Garden has 187 questions across four levels. Level 4 is the Stage 3 extension tier, built from the NSW EN3-6B outcomes and pitched at Excel Advanced Skills Year 6 difficulty: nominalisation, active and passive voice, main and subordinate clauses, simple / compound / complex sentences, relative clauses, adjectival and adverbial phrases, expanded noun groups, modality, text connectives, semicolons, colons, brackets and dashes, direct and reported speech, past perfect and present perfect tense, fewer / less, affect / effect, practice / practise, idioms, topic sentences, cohesive devices, redundancy and register. Puzzle Explorers now has five game types (including analogies — a classic fluid-reasoning exercise), and Sound Memory adds a BACKWARDS mode, which is the same working-memory skill tested in her assessment, plus a words mode.

🔁 No repeats

Grammar and Word Wizard now work through every question and word in a shuffled order before any of them come back, and they remember their place even after the app is closed. In Word Wizard, tapping ANY answer shows what that word means — so a wrong tap still teaches a new word, and the other choices are explained at the end of each round.

👤 Players & moving devices

Tap the player chip at the top of the home screen to add players — each gets their own stars, garden and levels on this device. To move progress to a new device or keep a safety copy, open the player screen and use the backup code buttons.

🔑 The three magic questions

Ask these whenever she's stuck — in the app or with homework:
1. "What information is important?"
2. "What is the first step?"
3. "Have you seen a similar question?"

💚 Praise effort, not answers

Say "You kept trying — that's brilliant!" rather than "You're so smart." The app never says "Wrong" — it offers a retry, then teaches the rule. Mistakes are counted as "watering the garden". Please use the same language at home.

⏱️ How to use it

10–15 minutes a day is plenty. Sit with her when you can and ask "How did you know?" after she solves something. For Sound Memory turn the volume up (or use "Show me instead"). Think & Talk cards are for the two of you together — any answer counts. Progress saves automatically on this device.
